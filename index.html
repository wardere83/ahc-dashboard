Program Spotlight — DRS Hiring + Faculty Mentorship

Allan Hancock College is piloting two interlocking initiatives — a Diversity Resource Specialist (DRS) program that embeds trained classified staff into hiring committees, and an equity-focused faculty mentorship model that trains four faculty mentors and a pilot cohort of eight mentees in culturally responsive teaching. Both are anchored at the Santa Maria campus and run through the Joint CCD’s Office of Human Resources and DEIA/EEO Committee.

GOAL 1Classified Hiring Equity

Pilot a classified-staff Diversity Resource Specialist (DRS) program to increase equity in hiring and diversify classified hiring committee participation.

Activities

  • Develop a DRS training handbook and toolkit.
  • Train 10 classified staff annually, representing each classified group family (craft/services, technical, clerical, professional).
  • Create sample one-pagers outlining equivalent skills for use in hiring committees.
  • Assign each trained DRS to serve on two hiring committees per semester.
  • Present training materials to DEIA/EEO Committee and CSEA for feedback.
  • Conduct data collection and analysis on DRS impact and classified hiring diversity.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

  1. 01
    20 classified staff trained over two years.
    AssessmentSign-in sheets, training evaluations.
  2. 02
    DRS serve on at least 40 hiring committees.
    AssessmentCommittee participation logs.
  3. 03
    Increased diversity awareness and reduced bias in hiring.
    AssessmentPre/post surveys, review of interview questions, and applicant pool diversity.
  4. 04
    Improved representation in CSEA hires.
    AssessmentCompare 2023–25 vs. 2025–27 CSEA hiring data.
GOAL 2Faculty Mentorship Model

Develop a faculty mentorship model to expand professional development and promote nontraditional career pathways.

Activities

  • Identify and train four faculty mentors in inclusive pedagogy and leadership.
  • Design a mentorship program focused on andragogy, curriculum design, classroom management, and reflective instruction.
  • Provide four training sessions for faculty mentees.
  • Support mentors and mentees in analyzing student-success data and setting instructional goals.
  • Offer cross-functional workshops for faculty, staff, and administrators.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

  1. 01
    Four faculty mentors trained and leading sessions.
    AssessmentTraining completion; mentor-developed materials.
  2. 02
    Eight faculty mentees complete training and implement changes.
    AssessmentSelf-reflection portfolios, revised instructional plans.
  3. 03
    20% of staff and 10% of faculty participate in at least one PD workshop per semester.
    AssessmentSign-in sheets, workshop evaluations.
  4. 04
    Improved student success in mentee courses.
    AssessmentDisaggregated student data (race / gender) before and after training.
How the goals connect
Application Narrative · Section 1.3

Together, these two goals form a cohesive, research-supported approach to advancing equity at Allan Hancock College: equitable hiring practices for the most demographically representative employee group on campus (65% BIPOC, 48% Latinx classified staff), paired with equity-minded instruction that improves outcomes for a student body where 70% identify as Hispanic / Latino, 59% are first-generation, and 24% are Pell Grant recipients (ed.gov / NCES College Scorecard).

Two Project Goals
From the application workplan, Section 2.1
GOAL 1 DRS for Classified Hiring
Pilot a Diversity Resource Specialist program for classified hiring

Train classified staff to embed equity advocates throughout recruitment, screening, and selection. Replicates the proven faculty DRS model for the largest, most demographically representative employee group on campus (65% BIPOC, 48% Latinx).

GOAL 2 Faculty Mentorship Model
Develop an equity-focused faculty mentorship model

Train four faculty mentors in culturally responsive teaching. Support a pilot cohort of eight mentees through curriculum design, inclusive andragogy, classroom management, equity-minded practices, and reflective instruction.

Workplan Activities
By project goal · from Section 2.2
GOAL 1 · DRS
  • Develop the DRS training toolkit. Comprehensive handbook focused on inclusive hiring practices, aligned with institutional DEIA / EEO goals.
  • Present to DEIA / EEO Committee and CSEA Executive Board. Refine through shared governance feedback.
  • Two annual training sessions. Up to 20 classified staff trained over two years (10 per cohort, representing all classified groups).
  • Deploy trained DRS members. Each serves on at least two hiring committees per semester.
  • Track CSEA hiring data. Pre/post analysis (2023–25 vs 2025–27), focus on Hispanic representation.
GOAL 2 · Mentorship
  • Train four faculty mentors. Culturally responsive teaching led by an equity consultant.
  • Co-develop the training framework. Five focus areas: inclusive andragogy, curriculum design, classroom management, equity-minded practices, reflective instruction.
  • Pilot cohort of eight mentees. Full training cycle including equity-focused course analysis, self-assessment, and instructional improvement plans.
  • Four formal training sessions. Plus mentor-mentee individualized plans, peer feedback, and outcome tracking.
  • Cross-functional workshops. 20% of staff and 10% of faculty target per semester.
Institutional Resources Used
  • Human Resources Department. Coordinates DRS training logistics; integrates equity checkpoints into hiring; partners with DEIA/EEO Committee and CSEA Executive Board.
  • DEIA/EEO Committee. Shared governance oversight and feedback on training materials and outcomes.
  • CSEA Executive Board. Labor partner for classified staff training and DRS deployment.
  • Office of Institutional Effectiveness. Data collection, disaggregated analysis, dashboards, and reporting infrastructure.
  • Existing faculty mentorship models. Faculty leaders and prior IBP grant experience seed the new cohort.
Five Faculty Focus Areas

The mentorship framework trains across five domains identified through AHC's 2023–2025 EEO IBP grant + CORA training:

  • Inclusive andragogy and pedagogy
  • Curriculum design reflecting student diversity and lived experiences
  • Classroom management and student engagement strategies
  • Equity-minded inclusion practices
  • Reflective instructional practice
