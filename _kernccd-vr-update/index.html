Program Spotlight - DRS Hiring + Faculty Mentorship

Kern CCD is launching a two-year VR Faculty Development and Peer Mentoring Program across Bakersfield, Cerro Coso, and Porterville. The program pairs structured VR pedagogy training with peer mentorship and closes a post-hire gap in Kern’s 2024-2027 EEO Plan by institutionalizing leadership and retention strategies.

GOAL 1Instructional Tech Access Across Colleges

Reduce instructional technology access gaps by ensuring faculty at smaller colleges (Cerro Coso & Porterville) have equal opportunity to experiment with and adopt immersive learning tools.

Activities

  • Stand up a districtwide cohort with seats proportional to each campus’s FTES so Cerro Coso and Porterville faculty have equal access to VR hardware and training.
  • Provision Oculus Quest 3 headsets and peripherals at all three colleges; share a common VR lesson catalog so material developed at one campus is usable at another.
  • Deliver six 4-hour VR pedagogy sessions in Fall 2026 covering hardware, best practices, inclusive lesson design, and collaborative curriculum development.
  • Pilot VR-enhanced lessons in Spring 2027 and present results at a districtwide end-of-year showcase.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

  1. 01
    At least 30 faculty trained across the three colleges, with participation proportional to each campus’s FTES (±5%).
    AssessmentSign-in sheets, cohort rosters disaggregated by campus.
  2. 02
    ≥ 35% of participants represent non-STEM disciplines.
    AssessmentCohort discipline tagging at enrollment.
  3. 03
    Searchable repository of ≥ 35 documented VR lessons, with ≥ 30% adopted across campuses (developed at one college, implemented at another).
    AssessmentStandardized lesson template completion logs & cross-campus adoption tracking.
GOAL 2Faculty Leadership Pipeline & Post-Hire EEO

Build faculty leadership through inclusive VR pedagogy and address a critical gap in Kern CCD’s 2024-2027 EEO Plan by adding sustained post-hire professional development and retention strategies.

Activities

  • Pair every cohort participant with a trained peer mentor for individualized guidance and troubleshooting.
  • Promote Year-1 participants into mentor roles in Year 2; introduce a VR creation track (Unity) and Faculty Innovation Stipends for larger-scale projects.
  • Equip faculty with VR-based strategies that foster empathy, cultural understanding, and engagement among diverse student populations.
  • Document leadership growth in participant portfolios (committee roles, coordinator duties, presentations) and feed results into Kern CCD’s EEO Plan refresh.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

  1. 01
    ≥ 75% of participants report increased confidence using VR; ≥ 60% implement at least one VR-enhanced lesson within the grant year.
    AssessmentPre/post participant surveys, lesson implementation logs.
  2. 02
    ≥ 40% of Year-1 participants advance to mentor roles in Year 2; ≥ 25% of alumni document new leadership/service.
    AssessmentMentor program rosters, professional-portfolio audits.
  3. 03
    In participating sections, ≥ 70% of students report increased engagement, with no engagement gap > 10 percentage points between student demographic groups.
    AssessmentPost-lesson student pulse surveys disaggregated by race / gender.
How the goals connect
Application Narrative · Section 1.3

Together, these goals form a single districtwide approach: equitable access to immersive instructional technology paired with a leadership pipeline that institutionalizes post-hire professional development. The program is anchored at Bakersfield College and replicated at Cerro Coso and Porterville so faculty growth no longer depends on campus assignment.

Workplan Activities
By project year · from Section 2.2
YEAR 1 · 2026-2027
  • VR training curriculum. Six 4-hour PD sessions in Fall 2026 covering VR hardware, pedagogy, collaborative curriculum design, and inclusive lesson planning.
  • Peer mentoring pairs. Each participant pairs with a trained peer mentor for individualized application of VR strategies.
  • Classroom implementation. Spring 2027 pilot of VR-enhanced lessons across the three colleges.
  • End-of-year showcase. Districtwide internal conference; standardized lesson documentation captured for the shared catalog.
YEAR 2 · 2027-2028
  • Mentor program expansion. New mentees plus returning participants advancing into mentor roles.
  • Advanced VR design. Returning faculty explore content creation tools (e.g., Unity) and pilot interactive experiences in Spring 2028.
  • Faculty Innovation Stipends. Larger-scale VR projects supported by stipends drawn from a dedicated pool.
  • Final showcase & living catalog. Districtwide presentation; documented lessons organized into a searchable, continuously updated repository across all three colleges.
Institutional Resources Used
  • Office of Planning & Educational Technology. Owns VR hardware procurement, faculty cohort logistics, and the shared VR lesson catalog.
  • Bakersfield College, Cerro Coso, & Porterville College. Each campus contributes cohort seats proportional to FTES and hosts on-site VR sessions.
  • Faculty leaders & VR subject-matter experts. Anchor the peer mentorship model and deliver the six-session training series.
  • District EEO Committee. Aligns the program with the 2024-2027 EEO Plan and shepherds the post-hire professional-development update.
  • Institutional Research / Office of Educational Technology. Data collection, disaggregated analysis, and lesson-catalog infrastructure.
Five VR Faculty Focus Areas

The training curriculum is structured around five domains drawn from the project workplan and CCCCO 2022 EEO/Diversity Best Practices Handbook:

  • VR hardware fluency (Oculus Quest 3) and classroom logistics
  • Best practices in VR pedagogy and immersive lesson design
  • Collaborative, cross-disciplinary curriculum development
  • Inclusive instructional design for diverse student populations
  • Faculty leadership growth - advancing into mentor and innovation roles
