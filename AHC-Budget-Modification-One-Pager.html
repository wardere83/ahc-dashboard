Expert Advisory — Read Before Submitting

  • Reconcile to exactly $100,000. A Tier 2 award is a fixed ceiling, so every modification must be a zero-sum reallocation. The most common filing error is entering the new $8,000 line without the offsetting reduction, which silently pushes the total to $103,000 (over ceiling). Confirm the sum of all Proposed Funds equals $100,000 before you submit — as modeled, it does.
  • A new expenditure type triggers prior approval and a workplan update. Adding a 5000 line that was not in the approved budget is a "substantial" change requiring CCCCO written approval (this form provides it). Because it introduces a new activity, also file the Grant Modification Request Form to align the workplan/outcomes — the dashboard flags this dependency explicitly.
  • Justify the 1000 → 5000 shift. Moving dollars from Instructional Salaries to a consultant/services line changes the personnel-to-services mix. Document that the consultant scope (planning + data analysis) is grant-allowable, non-supplanting, and procured per district/CCCCO rules (competitive quotes or a documented sole-source), with defined deliverables and a not-to-exceed rate.
  • Indirect (7000) stays valid — keep it that way. Indirect is $4,762 = 5.0% of the $95,238 direct base, at the EEO IBP cap. Because this is a like-for-like direct reallocation (total direct unchanged), 7000 needs no change. If the direct base ever moves, recompute so indirect never exceeds 5%.
  • Check benefits (3000) linkage. Releasing $8,000 of salary may reduce associated benefits. Confirm whether the discontinued stipends/position carried statutory benefits; if so, a small 3000 reduction could free additional funds (faculty stipends often carry minimal benefits, so the effect may be immaterial).
  • Watch the Year-2 back-loading. The change lifts Y2 spend +21% ($37,373 → $45,373). Ensure the ERG Conference and consultant deliverables land inside the period of performance (through Jun 30, 2028) and that Y2 staffing/cash flow can absorb the heavier load — back-loaded spend raises close-out and reversion risk.
  • Protect the funded goals. Confirm discontinuing the onboarding-stipend piece does not undercut Goal 2 (faculty mentorship: 4 mentors / 8 mentees) and that it is genuinely continued off-grant. Map the ERG Conference to an approved EEO outcome (employee resource groups → inclusive climate & retention) so CCCCO does not read it as scope creep.

Bottom line

  • Fundamentally sound: a balanced, well-motivated reallocation driven by a genuine staffing change — not a request for more money.
  • Approvable once (a) totals reconcile to $100,000, (b) a paired Grant Modification updates the workplan, and (c) the consultant procurement is documented.
