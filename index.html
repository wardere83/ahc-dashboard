Allan Hancock Joint CCD · Professional Development and EEO Representative Programs

Tier 2 Award

Two interconnected initiatives at Allan Hancock College. The first pilots a Diversity Resource Specialist (DRS) program to embed trained classified staff into hiring committees and promote equitable hiring practices. The second develops an equity-focused faculty mentorship model that trains four mentors in culturally responsive teaching and supports a pilot cohort of eight mentees.

Two Project Goals
From the application workplan, Section 2.1
GOAL 1 DRS for Classified Hiring
Pilot a Diversity Resource Specialist program for classified hiring

Train classified staff to embed equity advocates throughout recruitment, screening, and selection. Replicates the proven faculty DRS model for the largest, most demographically representative employee group on campus (65% BIPOC, 48% Latinx).

GOAL 2 Faculty Mentorship Model
Develop an equity-focused faculty mentorship model

Train four faculty mentors in culturally responsive teaching. Support a pilot cohort of eight mentees through curriculum design, inclusive andragogy, classroom management, equity-minded practices, and reflective instruction.

Workplan Activities
By project goal · from Section 2.2
GOAL 1 · DRS
  • Develop the DRS training toolkit. Comprehensive handbook focused on inclusive hiring practices, aligned with institutional DEIA / EEO goals.
  • Present to DEIA / EEO Committee and CSEA Executive Board. Refine through shared governance feedback.
  • Two annual training sessions. Up to 20 classified staff trained over two years (10 per cohort, representing all classified groups).
  • Deploy trained DRS members. Each serves on at least two hiring committees per semester.
  • Track CSEA hiring data. Pre/post analysis (2023–25 vs 2025–27), focus on Hispanic representation.
GOAL 2 · Mentorship
  • Train four faculty mentors. Culturally responsive teaching led by an equity consultant.
  • Co-develop the training framework. Five focus areas: inclusive andragogy, curriculum design, classroom management, equity-minded practices, reflective instruction.
  • Pilot cohort of eight mentees. Full training cycle including equity-focused course analysis, self-assessment, and instructional improvement plans.
  • Four formal training sessions. Plus mentor-mentee individualized plans, peer feedback, and outcome tracking.
  • Cross-functional workshops. 20% of staff and 10% of faculty target per semester.

Institutional Resources Used
  • Human Resources Department. Coordinates DRS training logistics; integrates equity checkpoints into hiring; partners with DEIA/EEO Committee and CSEA Executive Board.
  • DEIA/EEO Committee. Shared governance oversight and feedback on training materials and outcomes.
  • CSEA Executive Board. Labor partner for classified staff training and DRS deployment.
  • Office of Institutional Effectiveness. Data collection, disaggregated analysis, dashboards, and reporting infrastructure.
  • Existing faculty mentorship models. Faculty leaders and prior IBP grant experience seed the new cohort.
Five Faculty Focus Areas

The mentorship framework trains across five domains identified through AHC's 2023–2025 EEO IBP grant + CORA training:

  • Inclusive andragogy and pedagogy
  • Curriculum design reflecting student diversity and lived experiences
  • Classroom management and student engagement strategies
  • Equity-minded inclusion practices
  • Reflective instructional practice
